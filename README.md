## Thesis Summary

**Date:** March 2026 | **Data basis:** FY2025 audited financials

Pagaya facilitated $10.5B in network volume through 31+ lending partners (including SoFi, LendingClub, U.S. Bank, Ally), generating $1.30B in revenue and $371M in Adjusted EBITDA. The company achieved GAAP profitability ($81M net income) for the first time in FY2025.

### Key Investment Pillars

1. **AI network volume expansion** across personal loans, auto, and point-of-sale verticals
2. **Operating leverage** in a platform model where incremental volume flows through at high marginal profitability
3. **Asset-light transition** from balance-sheet-intensive funding to a fee model, de-risking the business

## Methodology

- 5-year DCF with Gordon Growth terminal value (WACC 13.0%, terminal growth 3.0%)
- Bottom-up operating model anchored in network volume growth and FRLPC margin dynamics
- Cross-checked with EV/EBITDA and P/E exit multiple valuations
- Scenario analysis across bull, base, and bear cases
